## 🧭 Executive Summary

A quantitative financial analysis of **Airbnb (ABNB)** stock from 2021–2024, benchmarked against the S&P 500 (GSPC). Using Yahoo Finance data, Excel modeling, and Power BI dashboards, we examined return performance, volatility, market sensitivity, and a forward price forecast to answer a core investor question:

> *Is ABNB worth the risk?*

**Verdict:** 11% annualized return with a beta of 1.58 and volatility more than double the market — high-growth potential, but only for risk-tolerant investors.

## 📈 Key Insights

> 💡 **ABNB volatility (0.45) is 2.25x the market (0.20)** — the return premium of 7% over risk-free rate doesn't fully compensate for that level of price swings for conservative investors.

> 💡 **Beta of 1.58 means ABNB amplifies market moves** — it rises faster in bull markets but falls harder in downturns. Strong positive correlation with S&P 500 makes it a momentum trader's asset, not a defensive hold.

> 💡 **Forecast confidence intervals widen significantly beyond week 55** — indicating high uncertainty and making ABNB unsuitable as a long-term predictable holding without hedging.

---

## 💼 Investment Outlook

**Short-term (Tactical Traders):** ABNB suits momentum-driven strategies — trade around earnings, travel demand data, and macro signals. High beta means amplified upside during bull runs.

**Long-term (Growth Investors):** Growth hinges on tourism recovery, regulatory environment, and macroeconomic cycles. Best held as part of a diversified portfolio alongside stable, low-beta assets to balance overall risk.

**Watch:** Quarterly earnings reports · Short-term rental regulation changes · Consumer travel trend data · Macroeconomic interest rate environment
